Introduction
Definition
The Wayfinder application allows managers of autistic employees to create task and subtask step cards designed to assist employees progress through workplace activities and to reduce cognitive load. The application features additional pieces of helper functionality intended to address several areas of autistic difference that have the potential to cause impairment in the workplace
Research Questions
  • Can software be utilized to lessen some of the inherent neurological differences preventing autistic adults ability to function in the workplace?
  • How effective are applications that focus on e-learning, project management, and job knowledge documentation in assisting autistic users complete workplace tasks?
User Overview
Anticipated Employee and Manager Goals

Employee Goals

  • Make it easier to complete work place tasks
  • Lessening of anxiety, having an easy place to review information, companion application
  • Inclusion, a tangible way to show that their team is committed to helping them succeed

Manager Goals

  • Teaching the user what to do in the workplace, giving them a knowledge base
  • Decreasing the amount of work on their end to help ASD employees succeed, path of least resistance
  • Promoting inclusion, having a tool that shows their commitment to making them feel like part of the team
ASD differences attempted to address

Executive Functioning

"the cognitive processes that help us regulate, control and manage our thoughts and actions. It includes planning, working memory, attention, problem solving, verbal reasoning, inhibition, cognitive flexibility, initiation of actions and monitoring of actions."

https://autismawarenesscentre.com/executive-function-what-is-it-and-how-do-we-support-it-in-those-with-autism-part-i/
“Some sources say that up to 80% of those with autism suffer from executive function disorder, leading to difficulties managing time, completing tasks, and making what for many of us would be simple tasks – like cleaning our rooms – very complicated or seemingly impossible.”

https://autismawarenesscentre.com/executive-function-what-is-it-and-how-do-we-support-it-in-those-with-autism-part-i/

Communication Differences

Difficulties in social communication in the workplace

Social Communication is "ability to share — in an appropriate manner — what you feel or want to say, and also to understand and respond to what others are feeling or saying."

https://www.spectrumnews.org/news/social-communication-autism-explained/
Further communication differences including:

Difficulty with figurative language such as idioms, metaphors, similes, and irony. Possible tendency to respond to suggestions, directions, or information in a very literal manner

https://www.iidc.indiana.edu/irca/articles/social-communication-and-language-characteristics.html

Visual Processing/Supports

"People on the autism spectrum tend to learn best using visual supports rather than through auditory input. Seeing it, rather than saying it, helps the person retain and process information."

https://autismawarenesscentre.com/visual-supports-best-way-use/
"Use these tools to create visual schedules for our folks on the spectrum because they create predictability which lessens anxiety."

Application Design
User Interface Design

General UI Design Choices

  • Neutral colors
  • Simple concrete icons or images to communicate redundant information with text, and accommodate multiple ways of understanding information.
  • Make content as short as possible without sacrificing precision and specificity, to reduce cognitive burden

Navigation

  • Simple, Consistent, Prominent
  • Helper Texts to give supplementary information on each page

File System UI

  • Simple, Consistent
  • Familiar, hopefully anyone that has used a computer will be familiar with this type of interface

Brand Identity

  • Tone/Voice in writing; hoping to convey a sense of inclusion, anxiety relief, informality
  • The app as a Wayfinder, like the ancient polynesian boat riders, helping users find their way in treacherous waters
Application Functionality

Tasks and Task Steps

  • Attempts to assist with executive functioning, specifically assisting users to step through activities, and help them understand how to reach the finish line of workplace tasks.
  • A repository for workplace knowledge.
  • Images and visual aids for increased visual learning.

Jargons

  • Attempts to assist with literal term definitions and how business context might change them
  • Increases independence, helps to limit potentially stressful verbal communication
  • Allows users to search workplace terms on their own

Jots

  • Attempts to address social communication differences.
  • A streamlined way to ask their manager questions and keep track of answers/context.
  • Use of emojis, further convey how they are feelings about a task, keep it light/informal, ok to be wrong.
Usability Testing

Testing Goals

Will attempt to assess if the Wayfinder application shed light on its research questions
  • Can software be utilized to lessen some of the inherent neurological differences preventing autistic adults ability to function in the workplace?
  • How effective are applications that focus on e-learning, project management, and job knowledge documentation in assisting autistic users complete workplace tasks?

Task Scenarios

  • Test participants will walk through prearranged example workplace task scenarios set up in the Wayfinder application.
  • These will be designed to test the functionality goals of several pieces of application functionality and design and collect task performance metrics.

Feedback Questionnaire

Test participants will then take a short questionnaire and self report their experience.
